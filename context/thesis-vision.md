# Thesis Vision & Context

## Project: Relics

A dark medieval fantasy open-world action RPG set in the **Shadow Realm** - a world scarred by ancient conflicts, inhabited by supernatural creatures (vampires, werewolves, otherworldly entities), and threatened by a disease called Schattenfieber (Shadow Fever).

**Game Engine:** Unreal Engine 5.6+
**Inspiration:** The Elder Scrolls (Skyrim), Vampire: The Masquerade, The Witcher 3, Gothic

## Thesis Title

**"Worlds Within Reach: AI-Assisted Worldbuilding for Independent Development"**

## Central Research Question

Can AI and procedural tools restore the creator-to-creation ratio that existed before games went 3D, enabling small teams to create open-world RPG experiences?

## Abstract

We are living through a technological revolution in media production. Artificial intelligence is reshaping how we create films, games, virtual environments, and interactive experiences at a pace that would have seemed impossible just a few years ago.

This thesis focuses on fun and creation - the joy of playing, and increasingly, the joy of being a creator. In the early decades of video games, small teams or solo developers could create titles that captivated millions. But the moment a game aspires to vast, detailed 3D worlds, team size explodes. Games like *The Elder Scrolls* require hundreds of people and budgets exceeding $100M.

The emergence of AI-assisted creation tools raises a tantalizing question: Can these technologies restore something of the creator-to-creation ratio that existed before games went three-dimensional?

This thesis explores that possibility using the open-world RPG as its lensâ€”because RPGs demand the full stack of worldbuilding: setting, characters, systems, narrative, and interactivity. It surveys AI-assisted creation tools and documents a practical attempt to build a prototype world.

**Scope:** Building a single biome proof of concept, documenting what works, what fails, and what remains beyond current tools.

## The Tension

AI can be a tool for exploitation or empowerment:
- Large corporations use it to replace artists and generate forgettable content
- Small teams might use it to realize visions that would otherwise be impossible

This thesis explores the second possibility.
